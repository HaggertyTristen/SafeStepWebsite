SafeStep | Key Takeaways

Insights & Lessons Learned

  • Data Augmentation Matters: Augmentation consistently improved validation accuracy and reduced overfitting, highlighting its importance in real-world noisy environments.
  • Batch Size Impact: Smaller batch sizes (16–32) led to faster convergence and more stable learning, but very small batches increased gradient noise.
  • Learning Rate Tuning: LR = 0.001 balanced speed and stability; lower rates required more epochs, while higher rates risked divergence.
  • Model Architecture: ResNet18 outperformed AlexNet and VGG16 slightly, indicating deeper models handle complex terrain patterns better, but require longer training and more GPU memory.
  • False Positives vs False Negatives: Minimizing false negatives is critical to safety. AlexNet achieved a low FNR (~0.03), making it suitable for high-risk scenarios.
  • Operational Recommendations: SafeStep performs best under diverse terrains and lighting when using augmented datasets. Regular retraining with updated field data is recommended.
  • Scalability: Prototype costs are manageable, and performance suggests potential for mass deployment in humanitarian and military operations.
  • Future Improvements: Incorporating sensor fusion (e.g., LiDAR + computer vision) could further reduce false positives, while additional architectures like DenseNet or EfficientNet may improve performance.
